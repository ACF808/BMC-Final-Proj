What Does Alcohol Do to the Immune System?

The immune system protects the body from bacteria, viruses, and other harmful invaders. Alcohol weakens this system by reducing the ability of white blood cells to fight infections and by increasing inflammation in the body.

White Blood Cells

Alcohol lowers the production and function of white blood cells, making it harder to fight infections.

Inflammation

Alcohol increases inflammation, which damages healthy tissues over time.

Gut Barrier

Alcohol weakens the gut lining, allowing harmful bacteria to enter the bloodstream.

Short-Term vs Long-Term Effects

Short-Term

  • Slower immune response
  • Higher risk of infections
  • Increased inflammation

Long-Term

  • Chronic immune suppression
  • Greater risk of pneumonia and TB
  • Delayed wound healing
